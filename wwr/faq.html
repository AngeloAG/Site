Frequently Asked Questions

Is Whitewater Rafting Safe for Kids?

Whitewater rafting with kids certainly is a fun and memorable family activity, and the risk for anything unfortunate happening is truly quite low. (According to American Whitewater, nationwide, the number of fatalities ranges between six and ten per year for an estimated 2.5 million user days on guided trips.)

How old my kids should be to go on a trip?

Size matters when it comes to paddling and staying in the raft. Generally speaking, kids under 12 are best suited for trips up to Class III. Twelve year olds and a little older up may be able to handle a Class IV challenge depending on the amount of paddling help that the guide may need in the rapids. Class V rapids should only be enjoyed by strong paddlers and individuals that can react quickly and help their raft get through the rapids safely - these rapids are for older teens only.
